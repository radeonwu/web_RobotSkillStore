What is RSS

A Platform for Validating and Deploying Robot Skills

RSS provides a structured pipeline to register, validate, certify, and deploy reusable robot skills — turning project-specific robot behaviors into long-term capability assets.

Core vision: As the App Store is to mobile devices, Robot Skill Store (RSS) aims to build a skill platform for robots in the physical world.

It enables robot capabilities to be standardized, productized, and traded — and reused and composed across different robots and scenarios.

Whom RSS aims to serve?

Robot Skill Store is designed for teams building the next generation of robots:

  • small and mid-sized robotics teams
  • industrial automation developers
  • embodied AI and agent researchers
  • platform builders exploring robot ecosystems

Where RSS sits in the ecosystem

RSS defines how robot skills are supplied, governed, and consumed across organizations.

  • Skill suppliers focus on capability development and publish skills.
  • RSS ensures validation and trust governance.
  • Users can deploy and reuse skills with confidence.
Supplier→ RSS→ User

Why RSS matters

RSS scales by compounding validated skills.

Today, robot capabilities are highly fragmented: “skills” are often buried in code, tightly coupled to a specific robot and environment, customized for a single scenario, and hard to reuse across teams or systems.

What we call a “skill” is frequently just a function call, a ROS node, or a one-off demo script.

This fragmentation leads to:

  • Poor sharing across robots and teams
  • Safety constraints not expressed structurally (hard to reason about systematically)
  • Sim-to-real transfer that does not scale
  • Orchestration that relies on hard-coded glue, limiting what agents can compose and decide

Robot Skill Store is designed to solve this root problem.
