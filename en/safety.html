Why Skill-Level Safety Matters

Safety mechanisms often focus on motion control, hardware limits, or model behavior. While necessary, these layers are insufficient on their own. Many failures occur because a skill is used outside its intended context, assumptions are violated silently, or constraints are implicit rather than explicit.

Skill-level safety addresses this gap.

Skills Define Their Own Boundaries

In Robot Skill Store, each skill explicitly declares operational constraints, environmental assumptions, and safety limits â€” as part of the executable specification (not just documentation).

This allows the system to:

  • Reject invalid executions early
  • Adapt behavior under constraints
  • Reason about risk before action

Policies and Verification

Safety is enforced through:

  • Constraints defined in skill metadata
  • Policies applied during selection and execution
  • Verification as part of the lifecycle

Before a skill is executed, its constraints are evaluated, its context is checked, and compatibility is verified. Safety becomes proactive rather than reactive.

Safety Across Simulation and Reality

The same constraints apply in simulation, during testing, and in real-world deployment. This consistency enables predictable behavior, reduces deployment risk, and accelerates safe iteration cycles.
