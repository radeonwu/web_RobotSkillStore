These three concepts are often conflated but represent distinct layers of robot software architecture.

Skill
A reusable, constrained execution primitive with explicit I/O, safety limits, and evidence. What RSS manages and certifies.
Task
A goal-oriented workflow that composes one or more skills under a plan. Tasks are defined at a higher abstraction level than skills.
Program
Implementation code that may contain ad-hoc logic, glue code, and environment assumptions. Programs are not inherently reusable or certifiable.

Implication: RSS focuses on turning program fragments into certifiable skills, so tasks can be composed reliably by humans or agents.
